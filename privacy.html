Privacy

It is possible to collect information shared on social media for further use. It is also possible that permissions have been allowed for the data to be used by third parties.
A presence created online about a person without their knowledge could impact their privacy. If you are sharing your own information you are aware of what has been shared, but what about your child? A parent is incharge of the online privacy of their children, but what happens when the children begin to use social media themselves.
Sharing about your children online is completely okay as long as you are aware of general security issues, practice good security. This should also be encouraged for children to do the same.

  • When you share on social media, be aware of how sharing your information could effect your privacy, or subsequently your childs future privacy.
  • It's alright to share information about yourself, though it is good to be in the habit of good security practices. Eg. your passwords shouldn't relate to something that can be found on your social media pages.
  • If you're worried about having complex passwords that aren't related to something in your life you'll remember, look into password managers.

"Two thirds of passwords are designed around one's personal characteristics, with most of the remainder relating to relatives, friends or lovers. Proper names and birthdays are the primary information used in constructing passwords, accounting for about half of all password constructions."
