Android Apps

There's an app for everything!

You can now track your pregnancy milestones, your baby's milestones, and you can connect with other parents in your area or across the world! All through using apps!

Two apps have been analysed, their primary purposes are social media for parents. Described are their uses, what using the app allows, and some of the hidden permissions of the apps.

Some people may use these apps to document their childs growth. Possibly considered safer than other social medias, or possibly just used to keep certain pieces of information seperate to other accounts.

Peanut

An app created to provide mothers with a social network to meet, interact, and chat with other mothers.
To meet others on the app, profiles appear of mothers within a specified radius, their profiles include chosen information about location, work, interests and information about their children.
You can share posts about your children; issues your having, milestones met, queries about parenting. You can also generally share about motherhood.
Imagine Tinder mixed with Facebook for mums.

When using this app you would often use:
Location
Camera - a selfie required to verfiy account creation
Read and Write to files, eg photos for sharing
Notifications
Has widgits to allow users to login to other social medias to follow/like the apps accounts

Permissions are what the apps are granted acccess to. This ranges from access to data stored on your phone, like contacts and media files, through to pieces of hardware like your handset's camera or microphone. Granting permission allows the app to use the feature.

This app is allowed to:

Accept cloud to device messages sent by the app's service.

View information about network connections such as which networks exist and are connected.

Open network sockets.

Access precise location.

Read from external storage.

Write to external storage.

Mush

Finding local mums in the area to meet up with and chat online. Specific section for advice. Uses your location to find mums in your are. Profile can describe work, interests, fun facts about the user, and basic information about their children.
Posts can be shared and can tag the topics it's about. This allows users to look through topics and find the posts that are relevant.

When using this app you would often use:
Location
Camera
Read and Write to files, eg photos for sharing
Notifications

Permissions are what the apps are granted acccess to. This ranges from access to data stored on your phone, like contacts and media files, through to pieces of hardware like your handset's camera or microphone. Granting permission allows the app to use the feature.

This app is allowed to:

Open network sockets.

Access precise location.

Access approximate location.

Required to be able to access the camera device.

Access information about networks.

Write to external storage.

Accept cloud to device messages sent by the app's service.
